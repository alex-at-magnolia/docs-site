Magnolia offers native A/B/n (or split testing) functionality to help you make decisions about your content based on real data from your existing traffic.

What is A/B/n testing?

The goal of testing is to determine which version of a page performs better among customers to increase your online conversion rates

A/B testing is a testing strategy in which two versions of a page (the original version A and a variant version B) are tested against each other.

A/B/N testing is an extension of A/B testing in which multiple versions of a page are compared against each other.

Start with a problem

Before designing a test, it’s considered good practice to identify a problem you want to solve and define a hypothesis. Existing problems are generally identified using analytics for your site.

Example:

  • Problem: Form completion for a free trial is very low despite high traffic on the page.

  • Hypothesis: By reducing the number of form fields from 15 to 5, we will increase the number of forms completed.

  • AB Test: Variant A uses the original 15 field form, variant B uses a new, shorter form.

  • The resulting conversion rates show that variant B performs better. This page can be selected as the winner.

Dedicated Testing app in Magnolia

Magnolia provides a marketer-friendly app to enable you to quickly create and manage your tests.

Clear overview

The Testing app gives you a single location where you can see all your tests. The columns show:

  • The variants involved in the test

  • The start date for the test

  • The state of each test (running, paused, etc.).

  • The conversion rate

Seamless variant selection

Seamlessly select the content you want to test from the Pages app. In the same app, you can choose your original variant and create a variation to test against directly.

Audience and allocation

You can use the Magnolia Segments to define specific audiences for your test and pick one or all of them in the Audience & Allocation tab.
This is also where you decide how much of your traffic you want to engage in the test.

Traffic allocation is set using the slider bar. Your visitors only ever interact with one variant.

Example:

  • You have 10 000 visitors to your specialist Kilt ecommerce site.

  • You have defined two segments: visitors from Scotland (8 000) and visitors from Ireland (2 000).

  • For your test, you select the segment Scotland as the audience and 50% traffic allocation.

This means that 4 000 Scottish visitors will be served the original variant A and 4 000 will be served variant B.
